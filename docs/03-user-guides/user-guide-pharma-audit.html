1. About This Guide

This guide is for Pharma API Manufacturers who operate as both buyer and supplier in the supply chain. As a buyer, you audit your upstream API suppliers. As a supplier, you receive and respond to audits from your downstream customers.

Your configuration: Pharma Audit module only (5 active modules). EQMS modules (Document Control, Change Control, Training, etc.) are disabled. You can enable them later without losing any data.

2. Platform Overview

HawkEye is a multi-tenant quality management platform that connects buyers, suppliers, and auditors through a structured 8-phase audit workflow. As a Pharma API Manufacturer, you'll use the platform in two modes:

🏢 As a Buyer

You initiate audits of your raw material suppliers, API intermediates vendors, and contract manufacturers. You assign auditors, track progress, review reports, and monitor supplier compliance.

Key pages: Request Audit, Audit Summary, FDA Dashboard, Supplier Risk

🏭 As a Supplier

Your downstream customers (formulators, brand companies) audit your manufacturing sites. You receive intimation letters, accept audits, respond to questionnaires, and submit CAPA plans.

Key pages: Audit Summary (supplier view), Questionnaire, DigiLocker

6. Workflow Guide: Buyer Role

6.1 Create a New Audit Request

1
Navigate to Request Audit
Click Quality → Request Audit in the top navigation bar, or go to /request-audit
Figure 4: Request Audit form — select supplier, product, site, and audit date
2
Fill the form
Required fields:
• Select Supplier — choose from registered suppliers
• Select Product — API product being audited
• Select Site — manufacturing facility
• Audit ETA — target date (must be ≥7 business days out, weekday only)

Optional fields:
• Intimation Letter Template — pre-fills the letter
• Pre-Audit Questionnaire Template — configures PAQ
• Artifact Requirement Checklist — mark which deliverables are mandatory
3
Submit
Click Request. The system auto-generates: a unique audit ID (HAWK0000000XXX), 6 draft artifacts (Intimation, PAQ, Scope, Execution Q, Findings Log, Final Report), and milestone tracking records.

6.2 Send Intimation to Supplier

4
Open the Audit → Artifacts tab
Find your audit in the Audit Summary list (/audits). Click it. Go to the Artifacts tab.
Figure 5: Audit Summary page — all audits with status, supplier, and phase
5
Send the Intimation Letter
Click Send to Supplier on the INTIMATION_LETTER artifact. This makes the audit visible to the supplier and sends a notification.
After sending: supplierVisible = true, trackStatus = "Audit intimation sent". The supplier can now see this audit in their Audit Summary.

6.3 Assign Auditor

6
Wait for supplier to accept
You cannot assign an auditor until the supplier has accepted the intimation. Status must show Supplier accepted intimation.
7
Assign auditor(s)
On the audit detail page, click Assign Auditor. Select an auditor and assign them as LEAD. You can also add CO-AUDITOR or REVIEWER roles.
Gating rule: Auditor assignment is blocked until the supplier has accepted the intimation. This follows the industry-standard "supplier-first" flow per PSCI guidelines.

6.4 Track Progress

Use the Progress tab on any audit to see the 8-phase stepper, milestone completion status, and SLA tracking. Each milestone shows who's responsible and when it's due.

6.5 Review Report & Close

8
Review the final report
After the auditor publishes the final report, review it. Set the Facility Outcome: Satisfactory, Conditionally Satisfactory, or Unsatisfactory.
9
Archive the audit
Once all CAPAs are verified, click Archive to close the audit and move it to the archived view.

7. Workflow Guide: Supplier Role

When your customers audit your manufacturing sites, you operate in Supplier mode. Here's what you do at each phase:

7.1 Receive Audit Notification

1
Check your Audit Summary
New audits appear in your Audit Summary page (/audits) once the buyer sends the intimation letter. You'll also receive an email/in-app notification.

7.2 Review & Accept Intimation

2
Open the audit and review the intimation letter
Read the audit scope, dates, and team composition. Review your options:
ActionWhen to useWhat happens
AcceptYou agree to the proposed dates and scopeCalendar reservation created, buyer can now assign auditor
Propose DatesThe dates don't work but you're open to the auditBuyer receives your proposed dates for renegotiation
RejectYou cannot accommodate this auditBuyer is notified; audit is blocked until resolution

7.3 Complete Pre-Audit Questionnaire

3
Fill the PAQ
Once the auditor sends the Pre-Audit Questionnaire, go to /audits/[id]/questionnaire. Answer each section. Upload supporting documents. Save as draft, then Submit when ready.

7.4 Respond to Execution Questionnaire

4
Fill the execution questionnaire
This is the main audit questionnaire. Questions are organized by category (Quality System, Production, Laboratory, etc.). For each question:
• Select Yes/No/Partial
• Provide explanation text
• Attach evidence documents (SOPs, records, certificates)
• Save as draft periodically
• Click Submit when all sections are complete

7.5 Submit CAPA Plan

5
Respond to each finding
After the auditor logs findings, you must provide a CAPA plan for each Critical and Major deficiency:
• Root cause analysis
• Corrective action with timeline
• Preventive action
• Responsible person
• Target completion date
Timeline requirements: Critical findings: CAPA plan within 15 days. Major findings: within 30 days. Minor findings: within 60 days.

8. Workflow Guide: Auditor Role

8.1 Accept Assignment

1
Review the audit in your list
Check your Audit Summary. Review the scope, supplier, and products. Accept or reject the assignment.

8.2 Prepare Scope & Agenda

2
Define scope and send to supplier
Open the Artifacts tab. Edit the SCOPE and AGENDA artifacts. Add your signature. Click Send to Supplier for supplier sign-off.

8.3 Build & Send Questionnaire

3
Select a template and send
Open the EXECUTION_QUESTIONNAIRE artifact. Select a template (e.g. "Full PSCI SAQ" — 98 questions across Quality, Production, Laboratory categories). The questions populate automatically. Review and customize if needed. Click Send to Supplier.
Prerequisite: Scope & Agenda must have supplier signature before the execution questionnaire can be sent.

8.4 Review Responses & Log Findings

4
Review each response
After the supplier submits, open the questionnaire in review mode. For each answer, you can:
• Accept the response
• Flag for follow-up
• Request additional evidence
• Log as a finding with classification

Go to the Report tab. The system can auto-generate a WHOPIR-format report using AI. Review the draft, add observations, collect signatures, and Publish.

11. Troubleshooting & FAQ

Q: I can't assign an auditor

The supplier must accept the intimation first. Check that supplierDecision = "ACCEPTED" on the audit. This follows the supplier-first flow mandated by industry practice.

Q: The supplier can't see the audit

You must send the Intimation Letter first. Go to Artifacts → INTIMATION_LETTER → Send to Supplier. This flips supplierVisible = true.

Q: The execution questionnaire is empty (no questions)

A template must be bound to the EXECUTION_QUESTIONNAIRE artifact. Go to the artifact detail page and select a template (e.g. Template 3: Full PSCI SAQ). Questions will auto-populate.

Q: I want to enable EQMS modules later

Go to /admin/module-config. Toggle on Document Control, Change Control, Training, etc. No data migration needed — modules activate immediately.

Q: How do I switch between Buyer and Supplier mode?

You don't switch modes. The same user can act as both. When you create audits, you're the buyer. When your customers' audits appear in your list, you respond as a supplier. The system routes based on who created the audit vs. who's the supplier.

Q: The page says "You don't have any audit"

Ensure you're on the Audit Summary page (/audits), not the role-specific placeholder pages. The unified Audit Summary shows all audits regardless of role.
