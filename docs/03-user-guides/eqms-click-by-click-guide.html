⚠️ Process 1: Report & Investigate a Deviation /nonconformance

Scenario: A temperature excursion is detected in Cold Storage Room A at 2 AM. The warehouse operator needs to report it immediately.

Step A: Report the Deviation

1
Navigate to /nonconformance
Menu: Quality System → Deviations / NCs
2
Click + Report Deviation
Top-right corner of the page. Opens the "Report Deviation" dialog.
3
Fill the form:
FieldWhat to enterRequired?
Title *Temperature excursion Cold Room AYes
Description *HVAC backup relay failure caused 4-hour temperature excursion above 8°C. Material at risk.Yes
TypeSelect: UnplannedNo (defaults to Unplanned)
Classification *Select: MAJORYes
Category *Select: EnvironmentalYes
DepartmentWarehouseNo
Area / LocationCold Storage Room A, Building 3No
Process StepMaterial StorageNo
Product NameMetformin HCl APINo
Immediate Actions TakenMaterial moved to backup cold room. QA notified. Area cordoned.No
4
Click Report Deviation
✓ Record created with auto-number DEV-2026-XXXX. Status: REPORTED Classification: MAJOR

Step B: Assess Impact

5
Find your deviation in the list. Click Assess
Button appears on records with status REPORTED or UNDER_ASSESSMENT
6
Fill the Impact Assessment dialog:
FieldWhat to enter
Product Quality ImpactPotential stability impact if excursion exceeded 4 hours. Affected batches: MET-2026-042, MET-2026-043.
Patient Safety ImpactLow — material not yet in production. No finished product affected.
Batch DispositionSelect: QUARANTINE
Regulatory ImpactNone — material quarantined before use in any batch.
7
Click Submit
✓ Status changes to UNDER ASSESSMENT

Step C: Investigate Root Cause

8
Click Investigate on the same record
Opens "Investigate: Temperature excursion Cold Room A" dialog
9
Fill the investigation:
FieldWhat to enter
RCA MethodSelect: 5-Why
Investigation Summary *Why 1: Why did temp rise? HVAC compressor stopped. Why 2: Why did it stop? Backup relay failed. Why 3: Why did relay fail? Relay was beyond service life. Why 4: Why not replaced? PM was 6 months overdue. Why 5: Why overdue? PM schedule not enforced.
Root CauseHVAC backup relay beyond service life — preventive maintenance schedule not enforced for cold storage equipment.
Root Cause CategorySelect: Equipment Failure
10
Click Submit
✓ Status changes to PENDING DISPOSITION

Step D: Dispose & Decide CAPA

11
Click Dispose
Fill: Disposition Decision = QUARANTINE
Justification = "Material quarantined pending stability retest. Relay replaced. PM schedule corrected."
✓ Status: PENDING CAPA DECISION
What happens next: The CAPA decision will determine if a formal Corrective & Preventive Action is needed. For MAJOR deviations, CAPA is almost always required. See Process 2 below.

🔍 Process 8: Initiate a Supplier Audit /request-audit

Scenario: Annual supplier re-qualification audit is due for Lupin (API supplier). QA Manager initiates the process.

1
Navigate to /request-audit
Menu: Audits → Request New Audit
2
Fill the audit request form:
FieldWhat to enter
Select SupplierChoose: Lupin Limited
Select ProductChoose: Atorvastatin (from Lupin's product list)
Select SiteChoose: Lupin Plant 2 (Aurangabad)
Intimation Letter TemplateOptional — select a pre-built template or leave blank
Pre-Audit Questionnaire TemplateOptional — select Template 7 or 8
Audit ETA *Select: a date ≥7 business days from today (weekday only)
3
Review the Artifact Requirement Checklist
Toggle "Required" for: Intimation Letter ☑, Scope ☑, Execution Questionnaire ☑, Findings Log ☑, Final Report ☑
4
Click Request
✓ Audit created: HAWK0000000XXX. 6 draft artifacts auto-generated. Status: "Request Created (Incomplete)"
5
Navigate to /audits/[id]/artifacts
Find the INTIMATION_LETTER artifact. Click Send to Supplier.
✓ supplierVisible = true. Supplier can now see this audit. Status: "Audit intimation sent"
What happens next (8-phase flow):
→ Supplier logs in, reviews intimation, clicks Accept
→ Buyer assigns auditor
→ Auditor prepares scope, sends questionnaire
→ Supplier fills questionnaire
→ Auditor reviews, logs findings, generates report
→ Supplier submits CAPA plan
→ Buyer reviews report, sets facility outcome, archives
See the full 8-phase flow in the Audit Flow Swimlane diagram.
